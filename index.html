Discidium

Founding Designer from October 2017 to June 2018

Discidium, now defunct, was an academically focused Machine Learning as a Service company that worked to provide tools to quickly and easily model and publish research data without the need to write code. This was done through a web-based client using AWS resources. Not only was I the UX designer for the full experience, but as the first and only designer at the company I had the opportunity to develop the design process and system, owning effectively every role in the design space from research to release.

We started our process by meeting with researchers at Stanford and data scientists at Google, gauging interest and learning about what services we could provide in our space. Once we got a solid grasp of the problem at hand and how we wanted to solve it, we slowly started to build up the design system and codebase from scratch. As the first simple pieces formed, the application grew into a fuller and more functional design powered by an increasingly complete backend. As we became settled on the design and front end development began in earnest, in an unfortunate turn of events, the company started to have financial issues and was forced to dissolve before the product could be completed. Working at Discidium forced me to confront many of my gaps as a designer, and even though in the end things didn‚Äôt work out as we‚Äôd hoped, it was an incredible learning experience and I‚Äôm glad I had the opportunity to really push myself and grow.

Product Designer from October 2015 to October 2016

Ascendify is an enterprise class HR software company that, at the time of my hiring, was focused on creating applicant tracking and recruiting systems for major customers including GE, Cisco, and Panasonic.
